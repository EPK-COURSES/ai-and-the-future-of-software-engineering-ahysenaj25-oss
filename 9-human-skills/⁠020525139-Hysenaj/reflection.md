# Personal Reflection: My Future as a Software Engineer

Conducting this research has deeply changed my perspective on my future career. Initially, the rapid advancement of AI coding assistants made me concerned about the long-term value of a software engineering degree. However, analyzing the industry trends has made me realize that while the mechanical act of writing code is changing, the core of software engineering remains deeply human.

What surprised me most was how critical emotional intelligence and communication are becoming. In the near future, being a good developer won't just mean knowing syntax; it will mean knowing how to ask the right questions, how to evaluate AI outputs critically, and how to bridge the gap between technical systems and human needs. 

Personally, I plan to adapt by focusing heavily on system design, data security, and collaborative projects. I want to embrace AI as a powerful tool to automate repetitive tasks, allowing me to focus my energy on solving complex, real-world problems that require genuine human creativity and ethical judgment.
